Troparion Cards

The hymns that define each feast — print them, learn them, pray them.

Home › Tools › Troparion Cards

Every feast day in the Byzantine tradition has two essential hymns: the troparion and the kontakion. The troparion states the theological meaning of the feast. What happened. Why it matters. The kontakion responds, often from the perspective of those who witnessed it. Together: a miniature catechism set to music, everything needed to understand a feast compressed into a few lines.

Below are the troparia and kontakia for the eight greatest feasts. Print them. Tape them to the prayer corner. Carry them during the feast season. They are meant to be memorized, not as rote exercise, but because these words, repeated day after day, teach the faith from the inside out.

🖨 To print: Use your browser's print function (Ctrl+P / Cmd+P). The cards are designed to print two per row on standard letter paper. Cut along the dotted lines.

The Nativity of Christ December 25
Troparion — Tone 4
Your Nativity, O Christ our God, has shone to the world the light of wisdom; for by it, those who worshipped the stars were taught by a star to adore You, the Sun of Righteousness, and to know You, the Orient from on high. O Lord, glory to You!
Kontakion — Tone 3
Today the Virgin gives birth to the Transcendent One, and the earth offers a cave to the Unapproachable One. Angels with shepherds glorify Him; the wise men journey with a star; since for our sake the Eternal God was born as a little Child!
The Incarnation: God enters creation through a cave, a mother, and the smallness of an infant. The star teaches the astrologers to worship not the heavens, but the One who made them.
Theophany January 6
Troparion — Tone 1
When You, O Lord, were baptized in the Jordan, the worship of the Trinity was made manifest. For the voice of the Father bore witness to You, calling You His beloved Son, and the Spirit in the form of a dove confirmed the truthfulness of His word. O Christ our God, You have revealed Yourself and have enlightened the world, glory to You!
Kontakion — Tone 4
Today You have shown forth to the world, O Lord, and the light of Your countenance has been marked on us. Knowing You, we sing to You: You have come and revealed Yourself, O Unapproachable Light!
The Baptism of Christ in the Jordan — the Trinity revealed: the Father's voice from heaven, the Son in the waters, the Spirit descending as a dove. The feast of light.
The Transfiguration August 6
Troparion — Tone 7
You were transfigured on the mountain, O Christ God, revealing Your glory to Your disciples as far as they could bear it. Let Your everlasting Light also shine upon us sinners, through the prayers of the Theotokos. O Giver of Light, glory to You!
Kontakion — Tone 7
On the mountain You were transfigured, O Christ God, and Your disciples beheld Your glory as far as they could see it; so that when they would behold You crucified, they would understand that Your suffering was voluntary, and would proclaim to the world that You are truly the Radiance of the Father.
On Mount Tabor, Christ's divine glory shines through His human body — "as far as they could bear it." The light the disciples saw is the same uncreated light that awaits all who pursue theosis.
The Dormition of the Theotokos August 15
Troparion — Tone 1
In giving birth you preserved your virginity; in falling asleep you did not forsake the world, O Theotokos. You were translated to life, O Mother of Life, and by your prayers you deliver our souls from death.
Kontakion — Tone 2
Neither the tomb nor death could hold the Theotokos, who is constant in prayer and our firm hope in her intercessions. For being the Mother of Life, she was translated to life by the One who dwelt in her virginal womb.
The "falling asleep" of the Mother of God — not death as ending, but passage into fuller life. She did not "forsake the world" — her prayers continue.
The Elevation of the Cross September 14
Troparion — Tone 1
O Lord, save Your people and bless Your inheritance. Grant victories to the faithful over adversaries, and by the virtue of Your Cross, preserve Your habitation.
Kontakion — Tone 4
As You were voluntarily raised upon the Cross for our sake, grant mercy to those who are called by Your name, O Christ God. Make all faithful people glad by Your power, granting them victories over adversaries by bestowing on them the invincible trophy, Your weapon of peace.
The Cross is not defeat but victory — an "invincible trophy" and "weapon of peace." The feast commemorates the finding of the True Cross by St. Helena in Jerusalem (4th century).
Pascha The Feast of Feasts
Troparion — Tone 5
Christ is risen from the dead, trampling down death by death, and upon those in the tombs bestowing life!
Kontakion — Tone 8
Though You descended into the grave, O Immortal One, You destroyed the power of Hades and rose as victor, O Christ God, calling to the myrrh-bearing women: Rejoice! And giving peace to Your apostles: Peace to you who have fallen grant resurrection.
The Resurrection — the center of everything. "Trampling down death by death": Christ enters death itself and destroys it from the inside. This troparion is sung hundreds of times during Bright Week.
The Ascension 40 days after Pascha
Troparion — Tone 4
You ascended in glory, O Christ our God, granting joy to Your disciples by the promise of the Holy Spirit. Through the blessing they were assured that You are the Son of God, the Redeemer of the world.
Kontakion — Tone 6
When You had fulfilled the dispensation for our sake and united earth to heaven, You ascended in glory, O Christ our God, not being parted from those who love You, but remaining with them and crying: I am with you, and no one will be against you!
Christ ascends — but does not leave. "United earth to heaven": human nature is now at the right hand of the Father. He remains with those who love Him.
Pentecost 50 days after Pascha
Troparion — Tone 8
Blessed are You, O Christ our God, You have revealed the fishermen as most wise by sending down upon them the Holy Spirit; through them You drew the whole world into Your net. O Lover of Man, glory to You!
Kontakion — Tone 8
When the Most High came down and confused the tongues, He divided the nations. But when He distributed the tongues of fire, He called all to unity. Therefore, with one voice we glorify the All-Holy Spirit.
Pentecost reverses Babel. Where the tower divided languages and scattered nations, the Spirit unites them. Fishermen become theologians — not by education, but by fire.

English hymn texts are sourced from publicly available translations and may differ from those used in your parish. This site is not an official publication of any eparchy, diocese, or church body.
