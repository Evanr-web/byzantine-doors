Icon Coloring Pages

Coloring pages based on iconic images of the Byzantine tradition. Print them, hand them to your kids, and let them encounter the faith through their hands. Each image is a real icon — not a cartoon. Children deserve to engage with the real tradition, even in simplified form.

The Holy Trinity

After the famous icon by Andrei Rublev (c. 1411). Three angels visit Abraham at the Oak of Mamre — a prefiguration of the Holy Trinity. The chalice at the center points to the Eucharist.

Our Lady of Częstochowa

The Black Madonna — one of the most venerated icons in the Slavic world. A Hodegetria-type icon showing the Mother of God pointing to Christ as the Way. The distinctive fleur-de-lis robes and crown mark this as the Częstochowa variant, deeply beloved by Ukrainian and Polish Catholics alike.

Christ Pantocrator

"Christ the Almighty" — the most fundamental image in Byzantine iconography, traditionally placed in the central dome of every church. His right hand blesses; His left holds the Gospel. The cross in the halo is unique to Christ.

Byzantine Doors
Catechetical and parish resources for the Byzantine Christian tradition
Byzantine Doors is an independent educational project created by a layperson to help English-speaking seekers and families discover the Byzantine Christian tradition. Theological content draws primarily from Christ Our Pascha, the catechism of the Ukrainian Greek-Catholic Church. English hymn texts are sourced from publicly available translations and may differ from those used in your parish. This site is not an official publication of any eparchy, diocese, or church body. For authoritative guidance on liturgical practice, fasting, and prayer, please consult your pastor.
