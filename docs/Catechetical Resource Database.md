# Byzantine Doors — Catechetical Resource Database

*Compiled April 30, 2026. All links verified unless noted otherwise.*

## How This Document Is Organized

This database is organized by source type. Each entry includes the URL, what's actually there, and whether it's **public domain** (free to excerpt/republish), **Creative Commons** (reusable with attribution), or **freely available but copyrighted** (can link to but not reproduce). This distinction matters for content creation.

## 1. CHURCH FATHERS — Public Domain Digital Texts

The 19th-century **Ante-Nicene Fathers (ANF)** and **Nicene & Post-Nicene Fathers (NPNF)** series are entirely in the public domain. These are the standard English translations and are hosted in full at two primary sites:

- **New Advent — Church Fathers Index**: https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/ (best indexed, easiest to navigate)
- **CCEL — Full NPNF/ANF Set**: https://www.ccel.org/fathers (bulk reference, downloadable)

### St. Basil the Great (c. 330–379) — NPNF2 Vol. 8
- On the Holy Spirit: https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/3201.htm
- The Hexaemeron (Six Days of Creation): https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/3203.htm
- Letters (300+): https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/3202.htm
- The Morals, ascetical works
- CCEL full volume: https://ccel.org/ccel/schaff/npnf208/npnf208.toc.html

### St. Gregory of Nyssa (c. 335–395) — NPNF2 Vol. 5
- **The Great Catechism** (Oratio Catechetica Magna): https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/2914.htm — *a reasoned explanation of Christianity for educated pagans; directly relevant for apologetics*
- On the Making of Man: https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/2916.htm
- On the Soul and the Resurrection
- On Virginity, Against Eunomius, Letters
- CCEL full volume: https://ccel.org/ccel/schaff/npnf205/npnf205.toc.html

### St. Gregory the Theologian / Nazianzus (329–390) — NPNF2 Vol. 7
- **Five Theological Orations** (Orations 27–31): https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/310227.htm through 310231.htm — *cornerstone texts on the Trinity*
- Defence of His Flight to Pontus (Oration 2) — on the priesthood
- Various orations, letters, poems
- CCEL full volume: https://ccel.org/ccel/schaff/npnf207/npnf207.toc.html

### St. John Chrysostom (c. 347–407) — NPNF1 Vols. 9–14
The most extensively translated Father. Six full volumes of homilies on nearly every NT book.
- Homilies on Matthew: https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/200101.htm
- On the Priesthood: https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/1905.htm
- Homilies on John, Acts, Romans, Corinthians, Galatians, Ephesians, Philippians, Colossians, Thessalonians, Timothy, Titus, Philemon, Hebrews
- Baptismal Instructions, Letters to Olympias
- CCEL Vols. 9–14: https://ccel.org/ccel/schaff/npnf109/npnf109.toc.html through npnf114
- *Chrysostom is an enormous reservoir of accessible, pastoral biblical commentary — all public domain.*

### St. Athanasius of Alexandria (c. 296–373) — NPNF2 Vol. 4
- **On the Incarnation**: https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/2802.htm — *arguably the single best patristic text for both Protestant and atheist/agnostic audiences*
- Against the Arians (4 discourses)
- Life of St. Antony: https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/2816.htm
- Defence of the Nicene Definition, Festal Letters
- CCEL full volume: https://ccel.org/ccel/schaff/npnf204/npnf204.toc.html
- Note: The popular C.S. Lewis-introduced edition (Penelope Lawson, 1944) may still be under copyright; the NPNF translation is fully public domain.

### St. Cyril of Jerusalem (c. 313–386) — NPNF2 Vol. 7
- **Catechetical Lectures** (Procatechesis + 18 lectures): https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/310101.htm — *actual 4th-century baptismal catechesis from Jerusalem; purpose-built for exactly what Byzantine Doors aims to do*
- **Mystagogical Catecheses** (5 lectures on the sacraments for the newly baptized)
- CCEL full volume: https://ccel.org/ccel/schaff/npnf207/npnf207.toc.html

### St. John of Damascus (c. 675–749) — NPNF2 Vol. 9
- **An Exact Exposition of the Orthodox Faith**: https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/3304.htm — *the great systematic theology of the East; the Eastern equivalent of a summa*
- **Three Treatises on the Divine Images**: https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/3301.htm — *the foundational theological defense of icons; essential for explaining to Protestants why icons are not idolatry*
- CCEL full volume: https://ccel.org/ccel/schaff/npnf209/npnf209.toc.html

### St. Irenaeus of Lyon (c. 130–202) — ANF Vol. 1
- **Against Heresies** (5 books): https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/0103.htm — *foundational for apostolic tradition*
- **Proof of the Apostolic Preaching** — a catechetical summary of the faith (J. Armitage Robinson translation, 1920, public domain; search Internet Archive)
- CCEL full volume: https://ccel.org/ccel/schaff/anf01/anf01.toc.html

### St. Ignatius of Antioch (c. 35–108) — ANF Vol. 1
- Seven authentic epistles (Ephesians, Magnesians, Trallians, Romans, Philadelphians, Smyrnaeans, to Polycarp): https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/0104.htm
- *Among the earliest post-apostolic writings. Profoundly important for ecclesiology, Eucharistic theology, and the episcopacy.*

### St. Cyril of Alexandria (c. 376–444) — NPNF2 Vol. 14 (partial)
- Five Tomes Against Nestorius, Christ is One, select letters
- Commentary on John (Pusey translation, 1874–85, public domain — search Internet Archive for "Cyril Alexandria Commentary John Pusey")
- CCEL: https://ccel.org/ccel/schaff/npnf214/npnf214.toc.html
- Note: Only partially translated into English even today.

### St. Maximus the Confessor (c. 580–662) — NOT in ANF/NPNF
- **Limited free availability.** No comprehensive free English translations exist.
- Some excerpts (Four Centuries on Charity, portions of Ambigua) circulate on Orthodox websites
- Internet Archive has borrowable copies of modern translations
- **Recommendation**: Purchase Popular Patristics Series volumes from SVS Press; use fair-use excerpts for content creation.

### St. John Climacus (c. 579–649) — NOT in ANF/NPNF
- The Ladder of Divine Ascent — the Archimandrite Lazarus Moore translation (1959) circulates freely online but copyright status is ambiguous
- Holy Transfiguration Monastery translation (1991) is under copyright
- **Recommendation**: Verify rights on Moore translation before formal publication; use quoted excerpts under fair use.

### Additional Apologetics Texts (all public domain, all on New Advent)

- **St. Justin Martyr** — First Apology: https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/0126.htm | Second Apology: https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/0127.htm | Dialogue with Trypho: https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/0128.htm — *Dialogue with Trypho is an actual model of respectful interfaith dialogue*
- **Epistle to Diognetus**: https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/0101.htm — *one of the most beautiful short texts in early Christianity; the "in the world but not of it" passage is endlessly quotable*
- **Clement of Alexandria** — Exhortation to the Greeks: https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/0208.htm — *directly addresses Greek philosophical objections*
- **The Didache** (Teaching of the Twelve Apostles): https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/0714.htm — *the most important early catechetical document in Christianity; shows the structure of early baptismal catechesis and Eucharistic practice*

## 6. DESERT FATHERS AND MONASTICS

### Sayings of the Desert Fathers (Apophthegmata Patrum)
Three major collections survive: the Alphabetic, the Systematic, and the Anonymous.
- **Early Church Texts — Apophthegmata Patrum (selection)**: https://earlychurchtexts.com/public/apophthegmata_patrum.htm — *small free selection with Greek and English; includes sayings of Anthony, Arsenius, John the Dwarf, Syncletica*
- **Internet Archive — Anonymous Sayings** (John Wortley, Cambridge UP, 2013): https://archive.org/details/TheAnonymousSayingsOfTheDWortleyJohn5090 — *available for borrowing but © 2013, NOT public domain. 765 sayings with Greek text and English translation.*
- **Benedicta Ward translation** (1975, Cistercian/Liturgical Press) — the standard Alphabetic Collection in English; under copyright, must purchase
- **Pelagius & John Latin translation** — 6th-century translation available in Migne PL 73 (public domain); the basis for Benedicta Ward's later English rendering "The Desert Fathers" (2003, under copyright)
- *The Desert Fathers sayings are perfect for social media, daily reflections, and bite-sized catechetical content. For public domain use, the best approach is to work from the NPNF-era translations of individual fathers (Cassian, Palladius) and the small selection on Early Church Texts.*

### Evagrius Ponticus (345–399)
- **Guide to Evagrius Ponticus** (Joel Kalvesmaki, editor): https://evagriusponticus.net/ — *the definitive scholarly hub; indexes all works, editions, and translations*
- **Fr. Luke Dysinger's translations** (freely posted online since 1990): http://www.ldysinger.com/Evagrius/00a_start.htm — *covers the Praktikos, Gnostikos, Skemmata, Chapters to Monks, Antirrhetikos (partial), and more. Not strictly public domain but freely available with apparent educational intent.*
- Key works with free English translations online:
  - **Praktikos** (100 chapters on the ascetic life): https://evagriusponticus.net/cpg2430/cpg2430.eng.1990.dysinger.html
  - **Gnostikos**: https://evagriusponticus.net/cpg2431/cpg2431.eng.1990.dysinger.tei.html
  - **Skemmata / Reflections**: https://evagriusponticus.net/cpg2433/cpg2433.eng.1990.dysinger.html
  - **Chapters to Monks**: hosted on evagriusponticus.net
  - **Antirrhetikos** (Scripture-based responses to the eight thoughts/temptations): Dysinger partial translation; full translation by David Brakke (2009, copyrighted)
  - **On Prayer / 153 Chapters on Prayer** (CPG 2450): traditionally attributed to "Nilus" in older editions; the Philokalia version is under copyright, but the Migne PG text (under pseudo-Nilus) is public domain
- **True public domain editions**: Migne PG (1857–1887, Latin translations under pseudo-Nilus attribution); Frankenberg 1912 edition on archive.org (Syriac with Greek retroversions); Gressmann 1913 edition of Ad monachos on archive.org
- *Evagrius's "eight thoughts" (the precursor to the "seven deadly sins") and his teaching on prayer are highly catechetically relevant. The Dysinger translations are the most accessible free English source.*

### John Cassian (c. 360–435) — PUBLIC DOMAIN
- **Conferences and Institutes** — NPNF2 Vol. 11: https://ccel.org/ccel/schaff/npnf211/npnf211.toc.html — *fully public domain*
- The **Conferences** are structured as dialogues with Desert Fathers on specific topics (discretion, prayer, friendship, the eight principal faults, etc.) — an accessible, conversational format ideal for catechetical adaptation
- The **Institutes** cover the external organization of monastic life and the eight principal faults in detail
- *Cassian is the bridge between East and West — he transmitted Egyptian Desert Father teaching in Latin. His works are fully public domain and cover nearly every topic relevant to the spiritual life.*

### Palladius — Lausiac History — PUBLIC DOMAIN
- Available in NPNF-era translations; contains biographical accounts of Desert Fathers and Mothers
- Includes accounts of Macarius, Pachomius, Evagrius, Melania the Elder, and many others
- *Useful for hagiographical/biographical content about the desert tradition*

## 7. ECUMENICAL COUNCILS — Texts and Canons

### NPNF2 Vol. 14: "The Seven Ecumenical Councils" — PUBLIC DOMAIN (Verified)
- **CCEL full PDF**: https://www.ccel.org/ccel/s/schaff/npnf214/cache/npnf214.pdf
- Edited by **Henry R. Percival, M.A., D.D.** (preface dated Pentecost 1899). ~1,165 pages. Fully public domain.
- CCEL states: "may be freely copied for non-commercial purposes"

**What's in this volume (confirmed from table of contents):**

**Creeds and Definitions:**
- The Nicene Creed (p. 41) + excursus on *Homousios*
- The Niceno-Constantinopolitan Creed (p. 328) + excursus on the Filioque
- The Definition of Chalcedon (p. 484) — Christological dogma
- The Decree on Icons from Nicaea II (p. 1020) + excursus comparing Latin and Greek teaching
- The Tome of St. Leo (p. 471)
- The XII Anathematisms of St. Cyril against Nestorius (pp. 392–413) + excursus on Theotokos

**Canons of the Seven Councils:**
- Nicaea I: 20 canons (pp. 49–108)
- Constantinople I: 7 canons (pp. 341–363)
- Ephesus: 8 canons (pp. 425–442)
- Chalcedon: 31 canons (pp. 493–542)
- Constantinople II: capitula and anathemas (pp. 568–583)
- Constantinople III: definition of faith on the two wills of Christ (p. 614)
- Quinisext / Council in Trullo: **102 canons** (pp. 632–752) — covers clergy marriage, fasting, icons, liturgical practice
- Nicaea II: 22 canons (pp. 1029–1056)

**Bonus — Provincial Synods and Patristic Canons (also public domain):**
- Council of Ancyra (25 canons), Neocaesarea (15), Gangra (20), Antioch (25), Laodicea (60), Sardica (20)
- African Code / Canons of Carthage: 138 canons (pp. 803–970) — sacramental discipline
- Apostolical Canons (p. 1083)
- Canonical epistles of St. Basil, St. Gregory of Nyssa, St. Athanasius, St. Cyril of Alexandria
- Extensive excursuses on: the Deaconess, the Chorepiscopi, early Church worship, vestments, minor orders, marriage of clergy, public penance, Pelagianism

**Catechetical value:** This single volume provides the doctrinal foundation for nearly every major teaching of Eastern Christianity — the Trinity (Nicaea I, Constantinople I), Christology (Ephesus, Chalcedon, Constantinople III), Icons (Nicaea II), plus the disciplinary framework of liturgical and sacramental life (Trullo). Percival's editorial approach is neutral and factual, making it reliable for catechetical adaptation.

### The Rudder (Pedalion) — Canons with Byzantine Commentary
- **Free PDF**: https://theoneholycatholicandapostolicchurch.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/The-Rudder-7-Ecumenical-Councils.pdf
- Based on St. Nicodemus the Hagiorite's 1800 Greek commentary with canons from all seven councils plus the Quinisext
- Format: each canon followed by interpretation citing the three great Byzantine canonists (Zonaras, Balsamon, Aristenos)
- **Copyright status unclear** — the original Greek is public domain (1800), but this English translation has no credited translator or license. *Use NPNF2 Vol. 14 as the primary public domain source; use the Rudder as supplementary reference.*

### New Advent — Ecumenical Councils
- https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/ (indexed within the Fathers section) — public domain NPNF translations

## 9. KEY GAPS — Opportunities for Byzantine Doors

Based on this research, the following are clearly underserved in the Anglo-Byzantine space:

1. **No centralized, modern, well-designed Eastern Catholic website for inquirers.** ByzCath.org is aging. Royal Doors is good but UGCC-internal. There is no Eastern Catholic equivalent of Catholic Answers or Desiring God.

2. **No free Eastern Christian "Bible in a Year" or structured Scripture program.** Fr. Mike Schmitz proved the demand. An Eastern version following the Byzantine lectionary cycle would be unique.

3. **No accessible digital lectionary tool.** Protestant audiences are used to Bible apps with daily readings. Nothing comparable exists for the Byzantine calendar that is polished and free.

4. **Icon-based catechesis is underdeveloped online.** Individual blogs exist but no systematic "learn the faith through icons" resource.

5. **Content bridging Protestant vocabulary to Eastern categories is scarce.** Most content assumes a Catholic or Orthodox starting point. Very little is written for the Baptist, Reformed, or non-denominational Christian who is curious.

6. **No Eastern Catholic catechumenate program exists online.** The RCIA-equivalent for Byzantine Catholics has no standardized, freely available digital format.
