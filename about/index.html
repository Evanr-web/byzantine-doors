About Byzantine Doors

Making Eastern Christianity accessible — one open door at a time.

What Is This?

Byzantine Doors is a free resource for anyone curious about Eastern Christianity — the ancient tradition of icons and incense, of chanted liturgies and golden domes, of monks in the desert and theologians who dared to say that humans can become God by grace.

Whether you're a lifelong Byzantine Christian looking for ways to explain your tradition to friends, a Western Christian discovering that your own heritage has an Eastern half, or someone who has never heard the word "Theotokos" and just wants to understand what's behind those golden doors — this site is for you.

Our Mission

Eastern Christianity is one of the richest spiritual and intellectual traditions in human history. It is also one of the least understood in the English-speaking world. Byzantine Doors exists to bridge that gap — not by simplifying or diluting the tradition, but by presenting it clearly, honestly, and beautifully.

We believe that good theology deserves good writing. We believe that ancient traditions can speak to modern people without being modernized. We believe that the best way to introduce someone to Eastern Christianity is not with a textbook but with an open door.

What We Are Not

Byzantine Doors is not affiliated with any single parish, eparchy, or jurisdiction. We are not an official organ of any church. We are not here to proselytize, recruit, or argue.

We are not anti-Western. Eastern and Western Christianity are two expressions of the same apostolic faith — two lungs of the same Body, as Pope St. John Paul II famously said. We present the Eastern perspective because it is underrepresented, not because it is superior.

We are not anti-Orthodox. Byzantine Doors draws from the shared heritage of all Eastern Christians — Eastern Catholic, Orthodox, and Oriental. The painful divisions between these communities are real, but the tradition they share is deeper than what divides them.

Our Sources

Content on Byzantine Doors is aligned with standard Eastern Christian theology, drawing primarily from:

  • Christ Our Pascha — the official catechism of the Ukrainian Greek Catholic Church, a comprehensive statement of Eastern Catholic faith
  • The Church Fathers — Athanasius, Basil, Gregory of Nyssa, Gregory of Nazianzus, John Chrysostom, Maximus the Confessor, John of Damascus, and others
  • Official liturgical texts — the Divine Liturgy of St. John Chrysostom, the Horologion, the Festal Menaion
  • Academic scholarship — peer-reviewed historical and theological research

Where theological opinions differ among Eastern Christians, we note the range of views rather than picking sides. Where the Eastern and Western traditions diverge, we present the Eastern perspective irenically — honestly, but without polemic.

Where to Start

If you're new here, we recommend The Journey — a sixteen-step guided path through Eastern Christianity, from your first encounter with the Divine Liturgy to the deepest questions of theology and practice. Each step is designed to be read in 5-8 minutes, and you can follow them in order or jump to whatever draws you.

You can also explore the Library for standalone deep dives on specific topics, or check the Tools & Guides for practical resources like "Your First Visit to a Byzantine Parish" and the Byzantine Fasting Guide.

Who’s Behind This

I’m a convert from Protestantism to Byzantine Catholicism in the Ukrainian Catholic rite. When I began my journey into the Eastern tradition, I found that while the Eastern Orthodox world has done a wonderful job building English-language resources for seekers, the Byzantine Catholic side of the tradition was far less represented. The resources were scattered, incomplete, or simply didn’t exist.

That gap matters, because I believe there is a deeply compelling case to be made for the Byzantine Catholic tradition specifically — a tradition that holds the full beauty and depth of Eastern Christianity while remaining in communion with Rome. As Pope St. John Paul II wrote, the Church must learn to “breathe with both lungs” — Eastern and Western. Byzantine Catholics live that reality every day. We are the proof that the ancient traditions of the East and the unity of the universal Church are not in competition. They belong together. And the English-speaking world deserves resources that reflect that.

I built this site because I wanted the next person on that path to have an easier time than I did.

Whether you’re trying to figure out how to pray with your kids in the Byzantine tradition, understand the difference between Eastern Catholic and Eastern Orthodox, make sense of what’s happening during the Divine Liturgy, or simply wondering what all the incense is about — I hope you find this site helpful on your journey.

This site is not intended to be authoritative. It is not a substitute for the guidance of your parish priest, your eparchy, or the living tradition of your local church community. It is meant to be a companion — a guide that points you toward the beauty of Eastern Christianity, which you will ultimately only know through participation. Go to Liturgy. Stand in the nave. Smell the incense. Receive the Eucharist. No website can replace that.

Always seek guidance from your parish priest in your own eparchy. And if something here helps you take one step closer to the altar — glory to God for that.

An Open Invitation

The doors of a Byzantine church are heavy. They are often made of dark wood, carved with crosses and saints, and they can look imposing from the outside. But they open. They have always opened. Everyone who pushes through them is welcome.

That is what this site is: a door. Not the destination — just the entrance. What you find on the other side is up to you.

Ready to walk through?
